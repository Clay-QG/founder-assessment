# Quiet Ground

## Operational Intelligence Diagnostic -- Source Document

### Purpose

The Quiet Ground Operational Intelligence Diagnostic is a short
founder-focused assessment designed to evaluate how clearly information
moves through a growing company and how easily leadership can understand
what is happening across the organization.

The goal is not simply scoring --- the goal is to surface operational
visibility, workflow clarity, and decision friction before introducing
automation or AI.

This diagnostic acts as the entry point into Quiet Ground advisory
conversations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Assessment Structure

### Target Audience

Founder-led companies with approximately **10--75 employees**.

Typical characteristics:

• growing complexity\
• leadership visibility beginning to decline\
• workflows evolving informally\
• automation or AI being considered

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Scoring System

Each question has four options scored as:

Very clear / Strong = 3\
Somewhat clear / Emerging = 2\
Limited visibility = 1\
Very unclear = 0

Total possible score:

10 questions × 3 points = 30

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Score Interpretation

## 24--30 --- Strong Operational Visibility

Information appears to move clearly through the organization. Leadership
likely has reliable insight into how teams are performing and how work
progresses across departments.

Organizations at this stage typically experience:

• Clear visibility into team activity\
• Faster decision-making with less leadership intervention\
• Well-understood workflows between departments\
• Strong conditions for introducing automation and AI

Leaders can spend more time focusing on strategy and growth rather than
operational troubleshooting.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 16--23 --- Emerging Operational Structure

Some systems, workflows, or reporting likely exist, but leadership may
still rely on manual updates, meetings, or individual knowledge to
understand what is happening across the business.

Organizations at this stage often experience:

• Partial visibility into team activity\
• Some workflow coordination, but inconsistencies between departments\
• Decisions that occasionally slow due to missing information\
• Early opportunities for operational improvements and automation

This stage is common for growing founder-led companies where complexity
begins to outpace internal systems.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 0--15 --- Limited Operational Visibility

Information may be fragmented across teams, tools, or informal
communication channels. Leadership often spends significant time
gathering updates, resolving repeated issues, or making decisions with
incomplete information.

Organizations at this stage frequently experience:

• Limited visibility into day-to-day operations\
• Repeated questions between teams\
• Delays caused by unclear workflows\
• Heavy leadership involvement in operational decisions

Improving operational clarity typically creates the foundation for
scalable systems and future automation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Diagnostic Categories

## Leadership Visibility

**Strong**\
Leadership appears to have consistent visibility into what teams are
working on and how the business is performing.

**Emerging**\
Leadership likely has partial visibility into operations, but some
information may still rely on informal updates or individual knowledge.

**Limited**\
Leadership visibility may be constrained by fragmented information or
inconsistent reporting.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Operational Flow

**Strong**\
Work appears to move through the organization in a reasonably structured
way.

**Emerging**\
Coordination may rely on informal communication or individual
experience.

**Limited**\
Workflow coordination relies heavily on ad‑hoc communication or
individual knowledge.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Decision Friction

**Strong**\
Decisions appear to be supported by reliable information and clear
ownership.

**Emerging**\
Some decisions move smoothly while others slow due to missing
information or unclear ownership.

**Limited**\
Decision‑making relies heavily on leadership intervention or incomplete
information.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Automation & AI Opportunity

**Strong**\
The organization appears open to exploring automation and AI where it
can improve operations.

**Emerging**\
Opportunities exist but workflows may not yet be structured enough to
fully support them.

**Limited**\
Automation opportunities are difficult to identify due to unclear
workflows or disconnected systems.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Assessment Questions

1.  How clearly can leadership see what is happening across the business
    right now?
2.  How clearly are responsibilities defined across teams?
3.  How easily can leadership identify operational problems when they
    occur?
4.  How consistently does work move between departments?
5.  How clearly are key business metrics tracked?
6.  How dependent is the organization on meetings for operational
    updates?
7.  How clearly are workflows documented across teams?
8.  How easily can new employees understand how work gets done?
9.  How frequently does leadership step in to resolve operational
    issues?
10. How prepared is the organization to introduce automation or AI?
